The problem

Sound familiar?

01

Sarah built a quote calculator in Claude. It's great. Saves her 20 minutes every time. It lives on her laptop. Nobody else can use it.

02

You asked IT to deploy it. The ticket is three weeks old. Sarah's already moved on to the next problem. The tool sits in her Downloads folder.

03

Meanwhile the sales team is still using a spreadsheet from 2019. It has 47 tabs, three broken formulas, and nobody remembers who built it.

How it works

Four steps. Thirty seconds.

No terminal. No git. No deployment pipeline. No waiting for IT.

1

Build with any AI

Claude, ChatGPT, Lovable — whatever you like. Ask it to make a tool. You get an HTML file.

2

Drag it onto AppHub

Open your team's dashboard. Drag the file in. Give it a name and an icon.

3

It appears in the grid

Your tool shows up instantly, right next to everyone else's tools.

4

Your team uses it

Click the icon, the tool opens. No accounts, no installs, no training.

Features

Simple by design

No feature bloat. Just the things a small team actually needs.

Secure & Private

Password-protected workspace. Every app runs in a sandboxed iframe with strict security headers. Your tools, your team, nobody else.

Your Brand

Your company logo, your colours, your name on the login page. It looks like yours because it is.

Team Visibility

See what your teammates have built. An activity feed shows who uploaded what. Building becomes visible and normal.

Phone-Style Grid

Apps arranged in a familiar grid with icons, just like your phone. Everyone already knows how to use it. Zero training.

Smart AI Uploads

Drop an HTML file and it works. Drop a JSX file, a zip, or raw LLM output? Pro converts it to a working HTML app client-side — no manual fixing.

Admin Control

Invite team members via email. Manage access, transfer admin. You decide who's in.

Why it matters

The real product isn't the portal

Something happens after the third person uploads their first app.

When a team member sees someone else using a tool they built, they go from "I made a thing" to "I'm someone who builds things."

When their colleague sees that and thinks "I could do that for my workflow" — that's the flywheel. It doesn't require a training program or an innovation workshop. It requires a place where building is visible.

The portal is the surface. The culture shift is the product.

"The best tools are built by the people who feel the pain."
